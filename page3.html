BUTTER CHICKEN RECIPE

Ingredients

  • Marinade
  • ¾ to 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • ¼ to ⅓ teaspoon salt
  • ½ to ¾ teaspoon Kashmiri red chili powder
  • ¾ to 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon kasuri methi
  • ⅛ teaspoon turmeric
  • ½ teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • ¾ to 1 tablespoon oil
  • ¾ tablespoon ginger garlic paste
  • ⅓ cup Greek yogurt or hung curd


  • Sauce/gravy
  • 2 tablespoon butter
  • ¾ tablespoon ginger garlic paste
  • 1 to 2 green chilies
  • 1 to 1½ teaspoon garam masala
  • ½ teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1 to 1½ teaspoon coriander powder
  • ½ tablespoon kasuri methi crushed
  • 1 cinnamon
  • 4 green cardamoms
  • 4 cloves

Recipe

  1. Use 500 g of boneless chicken, either thighs or breasts. Chicken thighs will give a richer flavor because of the higher fat content, while breasts are leaner. If using breasts, marinate longer for the best texture.
  2. Dry the chicken thoroughly with paper towels so the marinade sticks properly. Add chili powder, salt, and fresh lemon juice. Coat everything evenly and let it sit for about 20 minutes to absorb the seasoning.
  3. Add ginger-garlic paste, garam masala, cumin powder, coriander powder, dried fenugreek leaves, turmeric, and thick yogurt or Greek yogurt. Mix until the chicken is fully coated. Cover and refrigerate for at least 12 hours. Longer marination helps create softer, juicier chicken.
  4. Before cooking, remove the marinated chicken from the fridge and allow it to come closer to room temperature. This helps the chicken cook evenly.
  5. Heat a grill pan, skillet, or oven broiler over medium-high heat. Lightly oil the surface to prevent sticking.
  6. Place the chicken pieces onto the hot pan and cook in batches if necessary. Avoid overcrowding so the chicken develops a slight char.
  7. Cook the chicken for several minutes on each side until lightly browned and nearly cooked through. The edges should have a smoky grilled appearance.
  8. Transfer the cooked chicken to a plate and set it aside while preparing the sauce.
  9. In a large pan, melt butter over medium heat. Add finely chopped onions and sauté until soft and lightly golden.
  10. Stir in ginger-garlic paste and cook briefly until the raw smell disappears.
  11. Add tomato puree or blended tomatoes and cook until the mixture thickens and the butter begins separating from the sauce.
  12. Mix in chili powder, garam masala, cumin, coriander powder, and a small amount of sugar if needed to balance the acidity.
  13. Pour in a little water or cream to adjust the consistency of the gravy. Simmer gently until smooth and rich.
  14. Add the grilled chicken pieces into the sauce and stir well so they are evenly coated.
  15. Cover the pan and let everything simmer for several minutes so the chicken absorbs the flavors of the curry.
  16. Crush kasuri methi between your palms and stir it into the butter chicken for extra aroma.
  17. Add fresh cream and mix gently to create a smooth and creamy texture.
  18. Taste the curry and adjust salt, spice, or cream according to preference.
  19. Simmer for another 2 to 3 minutes on low heat until the sauce becomes silky and flavorful.
  20. Garnish with additional cream and chopped coriander leaves before serving hot with naan, roti, or basmati rice.
